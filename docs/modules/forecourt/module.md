## 1. Module Philosophy

### 1.1 Build on What Exists

The Forecourt module is **not a standalone silo**. A petrol station is at its core a retail business that receives goods (fuel deliveries), stores them (tanks), sells them (pump transactions), employs staff (attendants and cashiers), and reports financial results (GL). Every one of those activities has a home in an existing Awo ERP module.

The Forecourt module's job is to:

1. **Extend** existing modules with domain-specific concepts (a tank is a warehouse with extra physical attributes; a fuel grade is a product with density and temperature sensitivity; a cashier session is an HR shift with a till float).
2. **Orchestrate** the operational workflow unique to forecourt: meter readings, dip readings, shift reconciliation, and wetstock variance.
3. **Produce** the downstream artifacts that the Finance, Inventory, and HR modules consume — without duplicating their data.

### 1.2 What the Forecourt Module Does NOT Own

| Concern | Owned By |
|---|---|
| Fuel product catalogue (grade, unit) | **Inventory** — product with `category = FUEL` |
| Tank as a storage location | **Inventory** — warehouse/location with `type = TANK` |
| Fuel delivery receiving | **Procurement** — purchase receipt against a PO |
| Pump transaction as a sale | **Sales** — sales order / POS transaction |
| Cashier and attendant as employees | **HR** — employee record |
| Shift scheduling and attendance | **HR** — shift record, clock-in/out |
| General ledger journals | **Finance GL** — journal batch |
| Accounts receivable (credit customers) | **Finance AR** — invoice and receivable |
| Supplier management | **Procurement** — supplier record |

### 1.3 What the Forecourt Module Does Own

- **Physical infrastructure**: Pumps, nozzles, and their relationships to tanks and products.
- **Meter readings**: Cumulative totalizer values on nozzles — a concept that has no equivalent elsewhere in the ERP.
- **Dip readings**: Tank level measurements — physical observations unique to wetstock management.
- **Forecourt shift context**: The operational wrapper around an HR shift that adds a till float, meter snapshots, cash pickups, and a reconciliation.
- **Cash management events**: Floats, pickups, and safe drops that happen during a shift.
- **Wetstock reconciliation**: The computed comparison of theoretical vs actual fuel volume in a tank.
- **Fleet card operations**: Per-card fuel limits, usage tracking, and commercial account management.
- **Environmental compliance records**: Leak detection tests, spill incidents, and regulatory compliance tracking.

## 2. Domain Overview — The Forecourt Business

Before we model data, we must understand the business. A petroleum retail site is fundamentally a **volume business**. Revenue is generated by dispensing measured litres of fuel. Every business control — cash management, stock control, theft prevention, regulatory compliance — ultimately traces back to controlling and accounting for volume.

### 2.1 The Physical Site

A forecourt is organised as follows:

```
Site (Shell Maanzoni Service Station)
│
├── Tanks (underground storage)
│     Each tank holds exactly one fuel grade.
│     Tanks are the inventory source of truth for volume.
│
├── Pumps (dispensing islands on the forecourt)
│     Each pump island has one or more pump units.
│     A pump unit has one or more nozzles (hoses).
│     Each nozzle is connected to exactly one tank.
│
└── POS / Cashier Station
      Cash is collected here.
      Sales are recorded here.
      The cashier reconciles here at shift end.
```

### 2.2 The Money Flow

```
Customer pays
      ↓
Cash / MPesa / Card / Fleet Card collected at POS
      ↓
Expected cash = Volume dispensed × Unit price
      ↓
Cashier declared cash vs expected cash → Cash variance (over/short)
      ↓
Revenue posted to GL
```

### 2.3 The Fuel Flow

```
Delivery arrives (truck from depot)
      ↓
Dip reading BEFORE delivery
      ↓
Fuel pumped into tank (volume from truck docket + dip after)
      ↓
Stock increases in tank
      ↓
Throughout shift: customers draw fuel through nozzles
      ↓
Each nozzle's cumulative meter increases
      ↓
Volume sold = closing meter − opening meter
      ↓
Dip reading at shift end
      ↓
Theoretical stock = opening dip + deliveries − volume sold
Actual stock = closing dip
Wetstock variance = theoretical − actual
```

### 2.4 The Daily Operating Cycle

A petrol station typically runs 24 hours across two or three overlapping shifts. Each shift is the primary unit of accountability. The day is controlled not by the clock but by the shift handover. At every handover, physical readings (meters, dips, cash) are captured, handed from outgoing cashier to incoming cashier, and reconciled. This is the heartbeat of the forecourt business.

### 2.5 Why Variance Matters

A **wetstock loss** of even 0.5% on a site moving 50,000 litres per day is 250 litres — roughly KES 37,500 — every single day. Over a year that is KES 13.7 million in losses that may be from:

- **Evaporation and temperature effects** — normal and expected within tolerance.
- **Meter drift** — pump meters that under-read (dispense more than they record).
- **Delivery short-delivery** — supplier delivering less than the docket states.
- **Theft** — by staff (meter manipulation, short-fill) or by customers (drive-offs).
- **Tank leaks** — a regulatory and environmental emergency.

The wetstock reconciliation system is therefore not administrative overhead — it is the primary fraud and loss-prevention control for the business.

### 3.2 How Each Integration Works

#### Inventory Module — Products & Warehouses

The Inventory module is extended with two domain-specific types. No new product or warehouse table is created; instead the existing records gain forecourt attributes via extension tables.

**Fuel product** is an Inventory product where:
- `category = 'FUEL'`
- It has a corresponding `forecourt_product_attributes` row with fuel-specific fields (density, temperature correction factor, colour code).

**Tank** is an Inventory warehouse/location where:
- `location_type = 'TANK'`
- It has a corresponding `forecourt_tank_attributes` row with physical tank data (capacity, safe fill, ATG device reference).

All stock movements — deliveries in, sales out — are posted as standard Inventory stock transactions. The forecourt module triggers these; it does not own them.

#### Procurement Module — Deliveries

A fuel delivery is a **Purchase Receipt** in Procurement terms. The Procurement module owns the purchase order, goods receipt, and supplier invoice. The Forecourt module enriches the receipt with:
- Dip readings before and after delivery.
- Temperature-corrected volume.
- Delivery docket number and truck registration.

The Forecourt module listens for `procurement.goods_receipt.confirmed` events to update wetstock calculations. It does not duplicate the receipt data.

#### HR Module — Shifts & Staff

A forecourt shift is an **HR Shift** with a forecourt context wrapper. The HR shift owns shift start/end times, employee clock-in/out, and break periods.

The Forecourt module creates a `forecourt_shift_context` record linked to the HR shift, adding till float amount, opening/closing meter snapshots, cash pickup events, and the reconciliation result.

When the HR module closes a shift (employee clocks out), the Forecourt module is notified and initiates the close pipeline.

#### Sales Module — POS Transactions

Each fuel dispense is a **Sales transaction**. The Sales module owns the sale record, payment method, and customer (for credit sales). The Forecourt module adds the nozzle that dispensed the fuel, the volume dispensed (from meter delta or pump controller), and the shift context the sale belongs to.

#### Finance GL — Journals

The Forecourt module does not create GL journal lines directly. It calls the Finance GL service with a structured journal request at shift close. The Finance module owns the journal, the accounts, and the posting logic. The Forecourt module provides the business context.

## 4. Domain Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **ATG** | Automatic Tank Gauge. An electronic probe system that continuously measures fuel level and temperature inside a tank, typically polling every 15 minutes. |
| **Cashier Session** | The period during which a specific employee is responsible for a specific till. A shift may have multiple cashier sessions if the till is handed over mid-shift. |
| **Closing Dip** | A dip reading taken at the end of a shift or reconciliation period to determine actual closing stock. |
| **Dip Reading** | A measurement of fuel volume (or level in mm) inside a tank at a point in time. Can be manual (using a dip stick or calibration chart) or automated (ATG). |
| **Fleet Card** | A payment card issued to a commercial fleet account. Carries configurable fuel limits per vehicle or driver. |
| **Float** | The seed cash placed in a till at the start of a cashier session to enable making change. |
| **Meter Reading** | The value shown on a nozzle's cumulative totalizer at a specific point in time. Meters never reset; they are monotonically increasing. |
| **Nozzle / Hose** | A single dispensing outlet on a pump. Each nozzle is associated with exactly one tank and fuel grade. A pump may have two or more nozzles for different grades. |
| **Opening Dip** | A dip reading taken at the start of a shift or reconciliation period to establish opening stock. |
| **Pickup (Cash)** | The removal of excess cash from a till to a safe during a shift, to reduce theft risk. Performed by a supervisor and logged. |
| **Pump** | A physical dispensing unit on the forecourt. One pump may serve two sides of an island (hence multiple nozzles). |
| **Safe Drop** | The deposit of bagged cash into the site safe at the end of a shift. Requires dual control (cashier + supervisor). |
| **Shift** | The primary unit of operational and financial accountability for a forecourt. Corresponds to an HR shift but carries additional forecourt context (meters, dips, cash, reconciliation). |
| **Tank** | A physical underground or above-ground vessel that stores a single fuel grade. Represented in the ERP as an Inventory warehouse location of type TANK. |
| **Temperature Correction** | The adjustment of observed fuel volume to a standard temperature of 15°C, accounting for thermal expansion/contraction. Required for accurate wetstock reconciliation. |
| **Totalizer** | The cumulative litre counter on a nozzle. Records every litre ever dispensed through that nozzle since installation. |
| **Ullage** | The empty (unfilled) space in a tank above the fuel surface. `Ullage = Capacity − Current Volume`. Used to determine safe delivery volume. |
| **VCF** | Volume Correction Factor. Converts observed fuel volume at a measured temperature to the standard 15°C volume. Published by ASTM and OIML. |
| **Volume Sold** | `Closing totalizer − Opening totalizer` for a nozzle within a defined period. |
| **WAC** | Weighted Average Cost. The inventory valuation method applied to fuel stock. Recalculated with every delivery. |
| **Wetstock** | All liquid fuel held in tanks on the site — the "wet" stock as opposed to lubricants, shop goods, etc. |
| **Wetstock Variance** | `Theoretical closing stock − Actual closing stock (dip)`. Negative = loss; positive = gain. |

## 6. Data Model — Forecourt-Owned Tables Only

The following tables are owned and managed exclusively by the Forecourt module. All other data referenced lives in its home module.

> **Migration note:** The DDL sections below are ordered to satisfy foreign key constraints. `forecourt_shift_contexts` must be created before `forecourt_meter_readings` and `forecourt_dip_readings`, which both reference it.

### 6.1 Tank Attributes Extension

```sql
-- Extends Inventory locations that represent fuel tanks.
-- The canonical record (name, site, is_active) lives in inventory_locations.
CREATE TABLE forecourt_tank_attributes (
    location_id             UUID PRIMARY KEY REFERENCES inventory_locations(id),
    tenant_id               UUID NOT NULL,
    capacity_l              NUMERIC(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    safe_fill_l             NUMERIC(10, 2) NOT NULL, -- max fill without overspill risk
    minimum_operating_l     NUMERIC(10, 2),          -- low-stock alert threshold
    reorder_l               NUMERIC(10, 2),           -- reorder trigger level
    has_atg                 BOOLEAN       NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
    atg_device_ref          VARCHAR(100),             -- external ATG system identifier
    atg_manufacturer        VARCHAR(100),
    atg_model               VARCHAR(100),
    -- Tank physical properties
    tank_type               VARCHAR(30) DEFAULT 'underground', -- underground | aboveground | mobile
    tank_material           VARCHAR(30) DEFAULT 'steel',       -- steel | fiberglass | composite
    installation_date       DATE,
    manufacturer            VARCHAR(100),
    -- Calibration and correction
    calibration_chart       JSONB,        -- mm-to-litre lookup table if no ATG
    temp_correction_table   JSONB,        -- [{temp_c, factor}] pairs (ASTM/OIML VCF)
    last_calibration_date   DATE,
    next_calibration_due    DATE,
    -- Environmental compliance
    leak_detection_system   VARCHAR(50),  -- continuous | periodic | manual
    leak_detection_last_test DATE,
    vapor_recovery_system   BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
    spill_containment_l     NUMERIC(10, 2),
    -- Regulatory
    permit_number           VARCHAR(100),
    permit_expiry_date      DATE,
    created_at              TIMESTAMPTZ   NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
    updated_at              TIMESTAMPTZ   NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
    CONSTRAINT valid_tank_type CHECK (tank_type IN ('underground', 'aboveground', 'mobile'))
);
```

> **Why JSONB for the calibration chart?** Tank calibration tables are provided by manufacturers as mm-to-litre mappings — typically 500–1000 data points. Storing this as JSONB avoids a separate table while keeping it queryable when the application needs to convert a dip-stick reading in mm to litres.

### 6.5 Meter Readings

Meter readings are **append-only**. The totalizer on a physical nozzle is never reset; it only ever increases. The system must handle the rare case where a meter physically rolls over (returns to zero after reaching its display maximum).

```sql
CREATE TABLE forecourt_meter_readings (
    id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    tenant_id       UUID NOT NULL,
    nozzle_id       UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES forecourt_nozzles(id),
    -- Shift context. NULL only for out-of-shift spot readings (e.g. maintenance check).
    shift_context_id UUID REFERENCES forecourt_shift_contexts(id),
    reading_type    VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL,
    -- SHIFT_OPEN      — captured at shift start
    -- SHIFT_CLOSE     — captured at shift end
    -- SPOT            — ad-hoc reading during shift
    -- AMENDMENT       — correction of a previous reading (references superseded_id)
    -- CONTROLLER_SYNC — imported from pump controller system
    observed_at     TIMESTAMPTZ  NOT NULL,
    totalizer_l     NUMERIC(14, 3) NOT NULL,  -- raw meter display value
    rollover_count  INT           NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
    -- effective_l = totalizer_l + (rollover_count × site_rollover_max)
    -- Computed in application code, not stored, to keep readings immutable.
    source          VARCHAR(20)  NOT NULL DEFAULT 'MANUAL',  -- MANUAL | CONTROLLER | IMPORT
    recorded_by     UUID         REFERENCES users(id),
    superseded_by   UUID         REFERENCES forecourt_meter_readings(id),
    notes           TEXT,
    created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ  NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
    -- NEVER UPDATE. Corrections are new rows with reading_type = 'AMENDMENT'.
);
```

**Rollover handling:** When a 6-digit meter (max 999,999.999 L) rolls over, the technician records a new reading with `rollover_count = previous_rollover_count + 1`. The effective cumulative total is `totalizer_l + (rollover_count × 999999.999)`. Volume sold between two readings is always computed from their effective totals, never from raw totalizer values.

### 6.6 Dip Readings

```sql
CREATE TABLE forecourt_dip_readings (
    id                  UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    tenant_id           UUID NOT NULL,
    -- The tank (Inventory location)
    tank_location_id    UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES inventory_locations(id),
    -- Shift context if this dip is part of a shift open/close
    shift_context_id    UUID REFERENCES forecourt_shift_contexts(id),
    reading_type        VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL,
    -- SHIFT_OPEN         — opening dip for shift
    -- SHIFT_CLOSE        — closing dip for shift
    -- DELIVERY_BEFORE    — dip taken immediately before a delivery
    -- DELIVERY_AFTER     — dip taken immediately after a delivery
    -- ROUTINE            — scheduled ATG or manual routine reading
    -- SPOT               — ad-hoc verification
    observed_at         TIMESTAMPTZ   NOT NULL,
    -- Raw measurement. Either mm (from dipstick/ATG) OR litres directly.
    level_mm            NUMERIC(8, 2),            -- from ATG or manual dipstick
    volume_observed_l   NUMERIC(10, 2) NOT NULL,  -- converted from mm via calibration chart
    -- Temperature compensation
    temperature_c       NUMERIC(5, 2),
    volume_corrected_15c NUMERIC(10, 2),          -- NULL if temperature not measured
    -- Water bottom — >20mm triggers quality alert
```

## 7. Wetstock Management

### 7.1 What Is Wetstock?

Wetstock management is the discipline of accounting for every litre of fuel that enters and leaves the site. It is the fuel equivalent of stock control in a warehouse, but with additional complexity: fuel is a liquid affected by temperature, it evaporates, it can leak, and its volume cannot be measured with the same precision as solid goods.

### 7.2 The Wetstock Equation

```
Theoretical Closing Stock = Opening Stock + Deliveries − Sales

Variance = Theoretical Closing − Actual Closing (measured by dip)

Variance % = Variance ÷ (Opening + Deliveries) × 100
```

Every variable must be captured and controlled:

- **Opening stock** — from the previous shift's closing dip.
- **Deliveries** — from the Procurement module (goods receipt), reconciled against before/after dip readings.
- **Sales** — from the Sales module (POS transactions), confirmed by nozzle meter delta.
- **Closing stock** — from the shift-close dip reading.

### 7.3 Temperature Correction

Fuel volume is temperature-dependent. Petrol (PMS) expands by approximately 0.12% per °C. On a hot day in Nairobi where fuel is at 30°C vs the standard 15°C, a tank reading 10,000 litres at observed temperature holds approximately 9,820 litres at standard 15°C. Without correction, a site will report a consistent apparent "loss" in hot weather and a "gain" in cold weather, masking real variances.

The correction formula uses the **VCF (Volume Correction Factor)**:

```
Volume@15°C = Observed Volume × VCF(observed_temperature, fuel_grade)
```

VCF tables are published by the ASTM and OIML. Awo stores the relevant correction factors in `forecourt_product_attributes.temp_correction_table` as a JSONB lookup and applies them when `temperature_c` is captured on a dip reading. When temperature is not available, observed volume is used without correction but flagged accordingly in the reconciliation.

### 7.4 Delivery Reconciliation

When a fuel delivery arrives:

1. A **before-delivery dip** is taken (mandatory). This establishes the pre-delivery stock level.
2. The tanker pumps fuel into the tank. Start and end times are recorded.
3. After a settling period (typically 10–15 minutes), an **after-delivery dip** is taken.
4. Delivered volume = after dip − before dip (temperature-corrected).
5. This is compared against the **truck docket volume** (what the supplier claims was delivered).
6. If the dip-measured delivery differs from the docket by more than the configured tolerance (typically ±0.5%), the delivery is flagged as `DISPUTED` and the procurement goods receipt is placed on hold.

Steps 1–4 are captured in `forecourt_dip_readings` (with `reading_type = DELIVERY_BEFORE` / `DELIVERY_AFTER`). Steps 5–6 are handled by the Procurement module, which receives a `forecourt.delivery.volume_confirmed` event carrying both the docket volume and the dip-measured volume.

### 7.5 Variance Classification

| Classification | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **NORMAL** | Loss ≤ 0.3% | Auto-approved. Posted to GL as normal shrinkage. |
| **ELEVATED** | Loss 0.3%–0.5% | Shift marked for supervisor review before close. |
| **CRITICAL** | Loss > 0.5% or Gain > 0.3% | Shift `DISPUTED`. Manager must approve. Investigation record created. |
| **GAIN** | Any positive variance | Always flagged. Gains suggest delivery over-dipping or meter under-reading. |

Thresholds are configurable per product via `forecourt_product_attributes.max_loss_pct / max_gain_pct` and can be overridden at site level in preferences.

### 7.6 Integration with Inventory

When a shift is closed and the wetstock reconciliation is computed:

1. The Forecourt module publishes a `forecourt.wetstock.reconciled` event per tank.
2. The Inventory module consumes this event and posts a stock adjustment for the variance amount.
3. The stock adjustment carries a reference back to `forecourt_shift_tank_summaries` for full traceability.

This keeps the Inventory module as the single source of truth for stock on hand.

## 8. Shift Lifecycle & Workflow

### 8.1 Overview

The shift is the fundamental unit of accountability on a forecourt. Everything that happens — fuel dispensed, cash collected, deliveries received — must be attributed to a shift.

```
HR Shift Created (by HR module — schedule driven)
          │
          ▼
    forecourt_shift_context OPEN
          │
          │── Opening dip readings recorded (per tank)
          │── Opening meter readings recorded (per nozzle)
          │── Cashier session(s) opened with float
          │
          │   [During shift]
          │── Sales posted (Sales module → nozzle attributed)
          │── Deliveries received (Procurement module)
          │── Cash pickups performed
          │── Attendants rotated (if multi-attendant shift)
          │
          ▼
    Cashier initiates close
          │
          ▼
    Close pipeline runs (see Section 14)
          │
          ▼
    CLOSED or DISPUTED
          │
          ▼ (if DISPUTED)
    Manager review & approval
          │
          ▼
    GL journal posted (Finance module)
    Inventory stock movements posted
    HR shift closed
```

### 8.2 Shift Opening — Step by Step

**Phase 1: HR handoff.** The HR shift is already scheduled. When the employee clocks in via the HR module, the Forecourt module receives the `hr.shift.employee_clocked_in` event and creates or joins the `forecourt_shift_context` for that shift.

**Phase 2: Dip readings.** The supervisor or incoming cashier records an opening dip for every active tank. On ATG-enabled sites this can be automated. On manual sites it must be entered before the first sale is permitted.

**Phase 3: Meter readings.** Opening meter readings are recorded for every active nozzle. On sites with a pump controller, these are imported automatically.

> **Business rule:** The first nozzle sale of the shift cannot be authorised until all opening meter readings are recorded. This prevents the common fraud of a cashier beginning sales before capturing the opening reading, which would allow them to pocket early sales without detection.

**Phase 4: Float.** The cashier receives a counted float. The amount is entered and a `FLOAT_ISSUED` cash event is created. The cashier physically signs for the float.

**Phase 5: Shift is open.** The `forecourt_shift_context` status is set to `OPEN` and the first sale can proceed.

### 8.3 During the Shift

**Sales** are posted through the Sales module's POS interface. Each sale carries a `nozzle_id` and `shift_context_id`. The Forecourt module links to these records; it does not own them.

**Cash pickups** occur when the till cash exceeds a configured threshold (e.g. KES 20,000). A supervisor records a `PICKUP` cash event with a reference (envelope number) and removes the cash to the safe.

**Deliveries** are received through the Procurement module. The Forecourt module is notified and the before/after dip readings are recorded.

**Mid-shift attendant handover** — if a pump attendant is relieved, the outgoing attendant's record has `relieved_at` set, and a new record is created for the incoming attendant.

### 8.4 Shift Closing — Step by Step

1. **Closing meter readings** — all active nozzle closing readings recorded. Must be ≥ opening readings.
2. **Closing dip readings** — all active tanks. Automated on ATG sites.
3. **Cash count** — cashier physically counts the till and enters the total. `SAFE_DROP` event created, witnessed by supervisor.
4. **Other tender declaration** — MPesa, card, fleet, and credit amounts entered (or pulled from payment gateway reports).
5. **Initiate close** — supervisor confirms. Pipeline runs automatically.
6. **Review and approve** — if all variances within tolerance, shift closes automatically. If flagged, manager reviews before approval.

## 9. Cash Management & Cashier Accountability

### 9.1 The Till as a Cash Accountability Unit

Each cashier session is a bounded accountability period for a specific till. The golden rule is: **one cashier, one till, one session**. Sharing a till between two cashiers without a formal session handover makes it impossible to assign a variance to an individual.

### 9.2 The Cash Flow Equation

```
Expected Cash at Session Close
    = Opening Float
    + Cash Sales (payment_method = CASH, from Sales module)
    − Cash Pickups (sum of PICKUP cash events)
    − Cash Payouts (sum of PAYOUT cash events)

Variance = Actual Cash (counted by cashier) − Expected Cash

Positive variance = cashier over (surplus)
Negative variance = cashier short (shortage)
```

> **Important:** Credit, fleet, MPesa, and card sales are **excluded** from the cash expected figure. Each non-cash tender type is reconciled separately. The Stage 4 pipeline step (CASH_RECONCILE) must filter Sales transactions by `payment_method = CASH` only.

This equation is **computed at session close time** from its component records. Storing it earlier would allow it to be gamed.

### 9.3 Accountability Controls

**One cashier per session per till.** The `forecourt_cashier_sessions` table enforces that a session is linked to exactly one employee. If a relief cashier takes over mid-shift, the outgoing cashier's session is closed and a new session is opened for the incoming cashier with their own float.

**Dual control on pickups.** Every `PICKUP` and `SAFE_DROP` event requires both `cashier_session_id` and `authorised_by` (a different user with supervisor or manager permission). Application-layer validation must assert `authorised_by ≠ employee_id`.

**Per-cashier variance tracking.** The Finance module can query historical cashier variances to identify patterns — persistent short-changing, consistent surpluses suggesting under-ringing, etc.

**No direct cash refunds from the till.** Refunds are returned to the original payment method or issued as manager-approved vouchers. A direct cash refund requires an explicit `PAYOUT` event with an authorisation reference.

### 9.4 Credit Sales

When a credit customer fills up on account, the sale is posted through the Sales module with `payment_method = CREDIT` and linked to the customer's AR account. No cash is expected in the till. The reconciliation confirms that the credit amount matches the AR invoice raised in Finance.

### 9.5 MPesa and Card

Digital payments are treated similarly to credit for cash reconciliation purposes — no physical cash enters the till. The cashier declares totals at shift close and the system compares against the Sales module's records. Reconciliation against the Safaricom till statement and card terminal batch are separate Finance processes run daily and do not block the shift close.

### 10.2 Fleet Transaction Authorisation Flow

When a fleet card is presented at the pump:

3. **Limits check** — `current_day_l + requested_l ≤ daily_l_limit` and equivalent KES and monthly checks.

6. **Usage update** — `current_day_l`, `current_day_kes`, `current_month_l`, `current_month_kes` incremented atomically.
7. **Discount** — fleet discount (flat or volume-tier) applied and stored on the sales transaction. AR invoice raised by Finance at billing cycle end.

### 10.3 Reconciliation Impact

Fleet sales are included in volume reconciliation (they draw from the same nozzles) but **excluded from the cash expected figure**. The reconciliation records `fleet_sales_total` separately and confirms it matches the sum of fleet card transactions in the Sales module for the shift.

## 11. Reconciliation Engine

### 11.1 Two Separate Reconciliations

The shift close produces two distinct reconciliations:

1. **Cash Reconciliation** — Was the right amount of cash collected for the fuel sold?
2. **Wetstock Reconciliation** — Was the right amount of fuel sold (and received) compared to what the tanks show?

These cross-validate each other. A cash surplus combined with a fuel loss could indicate a nozzle that is under-reading while the cashier is collecting the correct customer amount — a meter fraud scenario.

### 11.2 Tolerance Configuration

Tolerances are site-level preferences. The defaults reflect typical petrol station industry standards in Kenya:

| Metric | Default Tolerance | Escalation |
|---|---|---|
| Cash variance | ±KES 50 absolute OR ±0.1% of expected (higher wins) | Supervisor approval |
| Wetstock loss | ≤ 0.3% of (opening + deliveries) | Normal — auto-close |
| Wetstock loss | 0.3%–0.5% | Supervisor review |
| Wetstock loss | > 0.5% | Manager approval + investigation record |
| Wetstock gain | > 0.3% | Manager approval (gains are suspicious) |

### 11.3 The Over/Short Report

At shift close the system produces a reconciliation report surfacing:

- **Sales by product** — volume, unit price, revenue, COGS.
- **Cash by tender type** — expected vs actual per payment mode (cash, MPesa, card, fleet, credit).
- **Wetstock per tank** — opening, deliveries, sales, theoretical close, actual close, variance.
- **Overall verdict** — balanced / requires approval.
- **Cashier summary** — per cashier session over/short.

This report is accessible to the manager before approval and is stored immutably after the shift closes.

## 12. Pricing & Revenue

### 12.1 Price Management

Fuel prices in Kenya are regulated by EPRA (Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority), which publishes a maximum pump price monthly. Individual stations may sell below this maximum. The Forecourt module does not own the price — it reads from the Sales module's price list.

A `Sales Price List` entry for a fuel product on a site becomes the forecourt selling price. The price is resolved at shift-open time using the Sales module's price resolution service and **locked per nozzle per shift** — a price change mid-day takes effect at the next shift open.

### 12.2 Multi-Tier Pricing

When `forecourt.price_tiers` is enabled, different prices apply to different customer categories:

- **CASH** — standard walk-in price.
- **CREDIT** — account customers (small premium for deferred payment risk).
- **FLEET** — high-volume fleet customers (discount off pump price, managed via `fleet_accounts.fuel_discount_pct`).

Price tier resolution is handled by the Sales module. The Forecourt module passes `customer_id` and `customer_type` to the Sales module's price resolver.

### 12.3 Revenue Recognition

Revenue is recognised at the point of dispensing. The Sales module creates the sales transaction; the Forecourt module confirms the nozzle volume. Both records must reconcile for the shift to close.

## 14. The Shift Close Pipeline

The close pipeline is a deterministic, ordered, idempotent sequence of stages. Each stage is a pure function: given the same input, it always produces the same output. This makes it safe to retry on failure.

Pipeline state is persisted in `forecourt_shift_contexts.pipeline_state` as JSONB after each completed stage. Example state after stages 1 and 2 complete:

```json
{
  "completed_at": {
    "READINGS_VALIDATE": "2024-11-15T07:05:00Z",
    "VOLUME_COMPUTE": "2024-11-15T07:05:01Z"
  }
}
```

On retry, any stage whose key appears in `completed_at` is skipped immediately.

```
POST /forecourt/shifts/{shift_context_id}/close
         │
         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 1 — READINGS_VALIDATE                              │
│ Check all active nozzles have SHIFT_CLOSE meter readings.│
│ Check all active tanks have SHIFT_CLOSE dip readings.    │
│ Detect meter rollover anomalies.                         │
│ Block if any reading is missing.                         │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 2 — VOLUME_COMPUTE                                 │
│ Per nozzle: volume_sold = close_totalizer - open_totalizer│
│ (rollover-safe). Aggregate per product, per tank.        │
│ Write forecourt_shift_product_summaries (draft).         │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 3 — PRICE_LOCK                                     │
│ Resolve price at shift-open time from Sales module.      │
│ Compute gross_revenue per product.                       │
│ Finalise forecourt_shift_product_summaries.              │
│ Emit forecourt.price.locked per product.                 │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 4 — CASH_RECONCILE                                 │
│ Compute expected cash (CASH payment_method sales only).  │
│ Exclude credit, MPesa, card, and fleet sales.            │
│ Compare against declared actual_cash_close.              │
│ Compute variance. Apply tolerance rules.                 │
│ Record credit, MPesa, card, fleet totals separately.     │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 5 — WETSTOCK_RECONCILE    [flag: forecourt.wetstock_reconciliation]
│ Per tank: fetch opening dip, deliveries, nozzle sales.   │
│ Apply temperature correction if flag enabled.            │
│ Compute theoretical vs actual closing stock.             │
│ Compute variance %. Classify (NORMAL/ELEVATED/CRITICAL). │
│ Write forecourt_shift_tank_summaries.                    │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 6 — APPROVAL_GATE         [flag: forecourt.variance_approval_required]
│ Evaluate all variances against tolerances.               │
│ If any CRITICAL: set status = DISPUTED. Halt pipeline.   │
│ Emit forecourt.shift.disputed event.                     │
│ On manager approval: resume from Stage 7.                │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 7 — INVENTORY_POST        [flag: forecourt.inventory_sync]
│ Emit forecourt.wetstock.reconciled event per tank.       │
│ Inventory module posts stock movement (sale out +        │
│ variance adjustment).                                    │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 8 — GL_JOURNAL            [flag: forecourt.gl_auto_post]
│ Build journal request (see Appendix A).                  │
│ Call Finance GL service.                                 │
│ Store gl_journal_batch_id on reconciliation.             │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 9 — HR_SHIFT_CLOSE                                 │
│ Notify HR module that the forecourt context is closed.   │
│ HR marks the shift as complete.                          │
└────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┘
                         ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Stage 10 — FINALISE                                      │
│ Set forecourt_shift_contexts.status = CLOSED.            │
│ Set closed_at timestamp.                                 │
│ Emit forecourt.shift.closed domain event.                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 14.1 Idempotency

Each stage checks `pipeline_state.completed_at[stage_name]`. If present, the stage returns success immediately without re-executing. This ensures a pipeline interrupted and retried does not double-post journals or double-emit events.

### 14.2 Amendment Flow

If a closed shift must be corrected (e.g. a cashier entered the wrong closing meter reading):

1. **GL reversal first.** If Stage 8 has already run, call the Finance GL service to post a reversal batch against the existing `gl_journal_batch_id`. This must happen before the pipeline re-runs, not after.
2. A new `forecourt_meter_readings` row is created with `reading_type = 'AMENDMENT'` and `superseded_by` pointing to the incorrect reading.
3. The shift status is set to `AMENDED` and `pipeline_state.completed_at` is cleared from Stage 2 onwards.
4. The pipeline re-runs from Stage 2. Stages 1 (already validated) remains marked complete.
5. When Stage 8 runs again, a new correcting journal batch is posted and linked.

All three journal batches (original, reversal, correction) are linked to the shift for full traceability.

```
  Total sales:                       1,503.950 L

WETSTOCK RECONCILIATION:
  Opening stock:                    10,069 L
  + Deliveries:                      8,000 L
  − Sales:                           1,503.950 L
  = Theoretical closing:            16,565.050 L

  Actual closing (dip @ 15°C):      16,431 L
  Variance:                           -134.050 L
  Variance %:                         -0.743%  (of opening + deliveries = 18,069 L)

  Classification:                   CRITICAL (> 0.5%)
  Action:                           Shift DISPUTED. Manager approval required.
```

This example illustrates why delivery reconciliation and wetstock reconciliation are interlinked. Disputes on a delivery should be resolved before the shift wetstock is approved, because an unresolved delivery dispute makes the variance figure meaningless.

### B.2 Fleet Card Transaction Volume Reconciliation

Fleet card transactions draw from nozzles exactly like walk-in sales. Their volume is captured in `forecourt_meter_readings` at the nozzle level and rolled into the tank's `sales_l` figure in `forecourt_shift_tank_summaries`. The fleet split (how many litres went to fleet vs cash vs credit customers) is visible in the `forecourt_shift_product_summaries` breakdown by `payment_method`, but does not affect the wetstock equation — litres dispensed are litres dispensed regardless of how they were paid for.
